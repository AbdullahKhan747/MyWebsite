About Me

I'm a biotechnology researcher currently pursuing my MS at Johns Hopkins University with a 3.9 GPA, expected to graduate in May 2026. My work focuses on cutting-edge gene editing technologies, particularly CRISPR-based base editing and lipid nanoparticle delivery systems.

At Johns Hopkins Medical Institute, I'm developing precision gene-editing approaches for Ataxia-Telangiectasia using LNP-mediated delivery in patient-derived iPSCs. My research combines molecular biology, nanotechnology, and bioinformatics to create clinically relevant therapeutic solutions.

My passion lies in translating laboratory discoveries into real-world applications that can improve patient outcomes and advance the field of precision medicine.
